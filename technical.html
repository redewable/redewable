Technical Brief

Campus, configuration, and stack · Phantom Hill 345 kV · ERCOT West

The Campus

Data center, battery, on-site generation — built around a cleared 600 MW interconnection.

ReDew Anson combines on-site natural gas generation, long-duration vanadium flow battery storage, and a cleared 600 MW envelope at Phantom Hill 345 kV — with the battery in-series between the grid and the data center load, behind an anchor tenant. The point of interconnection has been studied to 2 GW under normal conditions on official ERCOT base cases. The campus deploys as Phase 1 (74.5 MW of critical IT on a modular build) followed by three 200 MW phases; Phase 1 energizes mid-2028.

Battery Technology

Vanadium flow — long-duration, non-flammable, domestic.

i.
Chemistry
Vanadium redox flow. Non-flammable; cycles essentially indefinitely without capacity degradation. Electrolyte is reusable and recyclable.
ii.
Long Duration
Duration scales independently of power rating — configurable to anchor tenant firming profile and the ancillary services we'll bid into ERCOT.
iii.
Domestic & Low-Water
Domestically sourced electrolyte and stack components. Minimal water consumption vs. lithium-ion fire-suppression and cooling profiles. Safe for the community.
Configuration

In-series — battery between grid and load.

The campus runs a long-duration UPS configuration: storage sits in-series between the substation and the data center load; on-site generation feeds the load bus in parallel. The same battery captures grid arbitrage, sells ancillary services, and firms the tenant load — three revenue streams from one asset position.

Interconnection engineering by EPE (Electric Power Engineers), working directly with Lone Star Transmission, the transmission service provider at Phantom Hill. Specialized regulatory counsel will provide an independent legal opinion on the configuration as part of tenant and capital diligence.

On-Site Generation

Natural gas — funded, owned, and operated by our power-generation partner.

On-site natural gas generation provides firm baseload to the anchor tenant in parallel with the battery and grid supply. The fleet is funded, owned, and operated by our power-generation partner — generation capex and fuel risk sit on the partner's balance sheet, and power passes through to the tenant at cost on a triple-net basis. Generation is sized to the anchor's first-phase load and scales with subsequent phases across the 600 MW envelope. Air permitting for the on-site generation is being scoped; responsibility is expected to sit with our power-generation partner under the final structure.

Phase 1 of the data center itself is modular and prefabricated: factory-built blocks, assembled on site in roughly six to twelve months rather than a multi-year stick build. That is what makes a mid-2028 energization credible — and it holds construction cost meaningfully below traditional builds.

Site & Layout

Campus design.

Site control established across 168 acres at the in-service Phantom Hill 345 kV substation, minimizing gen-tie distance and substation BOP. Campus footprint prepared by Goree Architecture.
