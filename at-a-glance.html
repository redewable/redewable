The Project

A cleared 600 MW interconnection at Phantom Hill — built out as a data center and battery storage campus, anchored by a long-term data center tenant.

The core asset is grid capacity at scale. 600 MW of cleared injection at Phantom Hill 345 kV — a position the queue can no longer easily reproduce. We're developing that envelope as a data center plus on-site gas plus long-duration storage campus, energized in 25–50 MW tranches behind an anchor data center tenant. Hold and operate, not develop and sell.

Project Thesis

Why This Project

i.
The asset is the position.
600 MW of cleared injection capacity at a 345 kV node with strong grid metrics commands a reservation premium in the AI compute infrastructure market. The interconnection is the moat.
ii.
Firm power, not just clean power.
A battery firms power; it doesn't generate it. On-site natural gas plus grid plus long-duration storage is the firm-power story a sophisticated data center tenant can underwrite.
iii.
Phased, financeable, fillable.
25–50 MW tranches inside the cleared envelope. Each tranche is financeable on its own, fillable to a tenant's first phase, and monetizable from energization.
Configuration

LDUPS — battery in-series between the grid and the load.

The campus is configured as a Large-Duty Uninterruptible Power System. Storage sits in-series between the substation and the data center load. The same asset captures grid arbitrage, sells ancillary services, and firms the tenant's power — three revenue streams from one battery position.

1.
Grid arbitrage Charge during low-price hours, discharge into West Zone evening peaks and curtailment events.
2.
Ancillary services ERCOT regulation, ECRS, and DRRS — the new long-duration product — from the same asset.
3.
Tenant firming Battery and on-site gas together deliver tenant-grade uptime independent of grid state.

The LDUPS configuration is supported by deep ERCOT, Centerpoint, and Oncor relationships through our technology partner. We will also engage specialized ERCOT/FERC counsel for an independent legal opinion as part of tenant and capital diligence.

Why Here · Why Now

Why This Place

Grid Position
600 MW cleared at Phantom Hill 345 kV with strong SCR and no local thermal constraints. New entrants face a multi-year queue with no comparable position available.
Storage Deficit
ERCOT West is the highest-curtailment zone in the system. Jones County hosts 2.5+ GW of utility-scale solar operational or queued — storage demand at this node is structural.
Corridor Validation
ENGIE operates ~420 MW solar at Phantom Hill with 757 MW battery in queue. The Stargate AI campus near Abilene sits ~19 miles south. Hyperscalers, AI infrastructure operators, and IPPs are voting with their checkbooks on this corridor.
ERCOT West curtailment intensity and Jones County queued solar — the storage gap.
