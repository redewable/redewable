The Asset

Cleared 600 MW at a 345 kV node, ~19 miles from the largest AI compute build in North America.

ReDew Anson holds a cleared 600 MW envelope at the in-service Phantom Hill 345 kV substation, with site control established across 168 acres. We're developing it as an AI-grade data center plus long-duration energy campus — vanadium flow storage in-series between grid and load, on-site generation funded and operated by an independent power producer — anchored by a data center tenant. Phase 1 files as a small-load application directly with Lone Star Transmission, the transmission service provider at Phantom Hill: no ERCOT Batch process, no queue.

Capacity is the bottleneck. We control 600 MW of it, next to the corridor that needs it most.

Investment Thesis

Four facts the bet rests on.

i.
We don't stand in the queue.
226 GW of large-load requests sit in the ERCOT queue. Phase 1 doesn't join it — it files as a sub-75 MW small-load application directly with the transmission service provider, with studies of roughly nine months and a signed position targeted H1 2027. Cleared capacity plus a queue bypass is the moat.
ii.
AI compute has set the clock.
The Stargate AI campus near Abilene sits ~19 miles south. Hyperscalers and AI infrastructure operators are reserving capacity today for energization windows two-plus years out. The buyer is already in market.
iii.
LDUPS stacks three revenue streams.
Battery in-series captures ERCOT arbitrage, sells ancillary services, and firms the tenant's power — from one asset position. Hold-and-operate lets the operator capture all three. Develop-and-sell would surrender most of it.
iv.
Phased monetization derisks the bet.
Phase 1 — 74.5 MW of billable critical IT on a modular, prefabricated build — is independently financeable and monetizable, followed by three 200 MW phases under the cleared 600 MW envelope. Phase 1 economics close before full-envelope COD. No single-build cliff.

Structure

Block 1 SPV. Capital back first. Pro-rata into the envelope.

i.
Block 1 SPV vehicle
ReDew Anson Block 1, LLC — a single-purpose vehicle holding Phase 1 (74.5 MW of billable critical IT). ReDewable Energy Company serves as Managing Member with full operational control; investors hold passive membership units.
ii.
Capital-back-first waterfall
Invested capital is returned in full at the Phase 1 refinance, ahead of any distributions. Pro-rata distributions follow on a defined cadence post-COD. Construction financing — ITC transferability, C-PACE, senior debt — enters as debt, not equity, preserving the equity stack.
iii.
Pro-rata into future phases
Phase 1 investors hold pro-rata participation rights as the three 200 MW phases capitalize across the cleared 600 MW envelope.

Construction financing for Phase 1 is architected around the 40% ITC (transferability under §6418) and C-PACE anchored to closed-market precedent — the largest C-PACE deal closed to date is $465M. Modeled debt-service coverage runs 1.56x against a 1.25x covenant. Construction equity is repaid at COD.

Reg D Rule 506(b), accredited investors only; $50K minimum. Full terms in the Offering Memorandum.
