Where We Are

Two tracks · Phase 1 small-load with Lone Star Transmission · signed position H1 2027 · NTP mid-2027

Current Status

Feasibility complete. Two tracks. Signed position H1 2027.

ReDew Anson holds a cleared 600 MW envelope at Phantom Hill 345 kV, and the point of interconnection has been studied to 2 GW under normal conditions on official ERCOT base cases. The interconnection runs on two tracks. Track 1: Phase 1 files as a sub-75 MW small-load application directly with Lone Star Transmission, the transmission service provider at Phantom Hill — no ERCOT Batch process, no queue. EPE (Electric Power Engineers) is engaged and the application is prepared to file; Lone Star Transmission conducts the System Impact and Facilities studies, roughly nine months to a signed position in H1 2027, with NTP mid-2027. Track 2: the 600 MW envelope for Phases 2–4 files as one ERCOT large-load Batch application in 2027, running in parallel while Phase 1 builds.

The Configuration

Battery in-series — between the grid and the load.

The campus configuration places long-duration vanadium flow storage in-series between the substation and the data center load, with on-site generation serving the campus behind the meter. Because on-site generation carries the campus, the Phase 1 grid request stays under the 75 MW small-load threshold — which is what keeps the application with the transmission service provider and out of the ERCOT Batch process. Interconnection engineering is led by EPE (Electric Power Engineers), working directly with Lone Star Transmission. Specialized regulatory counsel will provide an independent legal opinion on the configuration as part of tenant and capital diligence.
