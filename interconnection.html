ReDewable Inquiry

Based on the limiting elements identified in the injection study, are you able—at a high level—to indicate the types of upgrades typically required in similar situations (e.g., reconductoring vs. substation work), and comment on whether the upgrade profile appears typical for a project of this size and POI?

Enerzinx Response

Based on the current study updates, the identified upgrade requirement is primarily Conductor upgrades. However, these results are sensitive to key assumptions, including system conditions and the assumed COD year and POI location, or system conditions are refined in subsequent study stages.

Key Takeaway

Conductor-based upgrades are favorable compared to substation modifications—typically faster to permit, less complex to construct, and representing the lower end of transmission network upgrade cost estimates ($30M–$80M range for a 300 MW project).
