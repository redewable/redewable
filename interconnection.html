The Position

A cleared 600 MW position at a 345 kV node.

ReDew Anson controls a cleared 600 MW envelope at the in-service Phantom Hill 345 kV substation (ERCOT Bus 68004, ERCOT West). Lone Star Transmission is the transmission service provider at the substation. Independent injection studies confirm 600 MW feasibility with strong SCR and no local thermal constraints at full capacity — and a newer transfer-limit study on official ERCOT base cases confirms the POI supports up to 2 GW under normal conditions. A position the queue cannot reproduce.

What the studies say.

i.
Transfer-Limit Study — 2 GW
A transfer-limit study on official ERCOT SSWG base cases (2029 MIN / 2030 SUM) confirms the POI supports up to 2,000 MW under normal (N-0) conditions. The 600 MW envelope sits well inside a much deeper bus.
ii.
Enhanced Feasibility (March 2026)
Updated injection study with the latest ERCOT SSWG base case and current queue conditions. 600 MW reconfirmed; no thermal violations for both charging and discharging scenarios.
iii.
Grid Strength
2,633 MVA SCR at the POI. Strong dynamic performance for storage and load. No comparable cleared positions available in the queue today.
Two Tracks

Phase 1 goes direct. The envelope files in parallel.

01
Phase 1 — Small-Load, Direct
Phase 1 files as a sub-75 MW small-load application directly with Lone Star Transmission. Because on-site generation carries the campus, the grid request stays under the threshold — no ERCOT Batch process, no queue. EPE (Electric Power Engineers) leads the application.
02
SIS / FIS Studies
System Impact Study and Facilities Interconnection Study conducted by Lone Star Transmission — roughly nine months, with a known interconnection cost of about $300K.
03
Signed Position → NTP
Signed interconnection position targeted H1 2027. NTP mid-2027; Phase 1 energized mid-2028 on the modular build.
04
Phases 2–4 — Batch Track
The 600 MW envelope files as one ERCOT large-load Batch application — three 200 MW phases — in 2027, running in parallel while Phase 1 builds. Construction-stage security is separately financed.

Why this matters: new large-load interconnections in ERCOT commonly take five to seven years, and 226 GW of requests sit in the large-load queue today. Phase 1 doesn't stand in that line. That is the difference between a project that talks about 2028 and one that can deliver it.
