Regulatory Compliance

NFT/Token Structure ‚Äî Utility, Not Security

The Validator License NFT is structured as a utility token, not a security. This enables broad participation ‚Äî anyone globally can purchase an NFT, regardless of accreditation status.

What is the Howey Test?

The Howey Test is a legal test established by the U.S. Supreme Court (SEC v. W.J. Howey Co., 1946) to determine whether a transaction qualifies as an "investment contract" ‚Äî and therefore a security subject to SEC regulation. If all four prongs are met, the asset is a security and must be registered with the SEC or sold under an exemption. By structuring our NFT to fail key prongs, we avoid securities classification entirely.

Howey Test Analysis

Howey Prong Our Position Analysis
1. Investment of money Yes Buyers pay SOL ‚Äî this prong is met
2. Common enterprise Mitigated Decentralized validator network; rewards vary by individual participation
3. Expectation of profits NO Rewards are compensation for work performed (attestations), not passive investment returns
4. Efforts of others NO Validators must actively participate monthly; rewards depend on their own work

Why This Matters: Open Access

Because the NFT is not a security, it can be sold to anyone ‚Äî no accreditation required, no income verification, no net worth checks. A teacher in Texas, a developer in Germany, or a student in Japan can all participate equally. This is the power of proper token structuring: regulatory clarity enables global, permissionless participation.

Key Protections

  • Active participation required: Monthly attestations are mandatory to earn rewards
  • Work-based rewards: Earnings explicitly tied to validator activity, not passive holding
  • No profit language: All marketing emphasizes utility and participation, never investment returns
  • Clear disclaimers: Terms of service and purchase flow include prominent risk disclosures
  • Helium precedent: SEC dropped its case against Helium (April 2025) with prejudice, establishing that hardware + token rewards for network participation ‚â† securities
