02 · Position

The interconnection is the asset. Everything else can be built.

New 345 kV interconnects in ERCOT take five to seven years. We hold a studied, energized position that already clears 600 MW.

The largest data-center build-out in history is happening in our corridor. The Stargate clean-energy campus sits ~20 miles south; Vantage Data Centers' Frontier campus ~30 miles east. Around us is gigawatts of solar that the West Zone increasingly curtails — generation looking for firming and load looking for power, meeting at one of the few interconnection points that can host the answer.

Why this corridor, why now
The West Load Zone curtails solar at a rate that grows every quarter while ~10 GW of 24/7 clean-firm demand arrives. The infrastructure that exists underestimated what is coming. The interconnection points that can host firmed, long-duration capacity are scarce — and we have one of them.

Phase 1 (74.5 MW critical IT, modular) leads; three 200 MW phases follow under the 600 MW envelope — each independently financeable, fillable, and monetizable.

What the intermediate constraints mean

The study flags incremental loading on neighboring 138 kV facilities in the AEP_TCC and CNP_TSP areas at intermediate steps (~340–470 MW discharge; ~80–220 MW charge). These are driven by queued projects in the vicinity, not by ReDew — the study explicitly notes queue-driven upgrade costs should not be allocated to the proposed interconnection. At the full 600 MW target, both directions clear. Net read: a strong bus with an addressable, queue-sensitive upgrade picture.

Open design + regulatory workstream for onboarding
The Enerzinx study is a steady-state thermal injection study (standalone storage, ±10% informational). TerraFlow's own transfer-limit study goes further: run on official ERCOT SSWG base cases (2029 MIN / 2030 SUM), it confirms the POI supports up to 2,000 MW under normal (N-0) conditions. On the application side, Phase 1 files as a sub-75 MW small-load request directly with Lone Star Transmission — EPE (Electric Power Engineers) leads, working directly with the TSP, with the path confirmed in writing. The LDUPS in-series topology and its co-location structure remain a deliberate joint-design workstream. Confirming the interconnection is export-capable is a gating item — the arbitrage revenue depends on it.

The order book

Phase 1 carries a ~$465M turnkey vanadium-flow purchase order at firm pricing. Each phase is independently financeable and fillable, so the cleared 600 MW envelope — studied to 2 GW — scales TerraFlow deployment across three further 200 MW phases. One cleared interconnection, a campus of orders.

What makes the order firm — the bankability chain

  • TerraFlow design + LDUPS simulation — the revenue model the lender and tenant underwrite.
  • Interconnection engineering (LST small-load → signed position, EPE-led) — the deliverable position behind the campus.
  • Anchor tenant contract — the contractual floor that sets the exit valuation tier.
  • Signed IA + NTP — Block 1 battery order firm; ReDew capitalizes and de-risks the pre-development path to get there.

Phase 1 base-case economics (model output): total development cost ~$1.40B; stabilized NOI ~$147.9M (a 10.6% yield); modeled DSCR of 1.56x against a 1.25x covenant. The structure — 40% ITC transferability plus C-PACE anchored to the $465M closed-market precedent plus senior debt — is built so construction equity is repaid at COD. ReDewable carries the pre-development arc: a $3.55M pre-NTP program (~$1.1M committed, $2.45M offered) covers the work between today and a signed position / NTP, at which point Phase 1 capitalizes and the battery order becomes firm. A bankable project is what makes the battery order real.
