Environmental & Permitting Assessment

ReDew Anson — Jones County, Texas
Due Diligence Summary · January 2026
ESE Partners Tetra Tech

Executive Summary

Based on preliminary desktop review and consultations with environmental firms, the ReDew Anson site presents a favorable environmental profile. The property is located directly adjacent to ENGIE's operational Anson Solar Farm, which has already undergone comprehensive environmental review. The site consists of agricultural land (cotton fields) in a drought-prone region with no apparent wetlands, flood zones, or sensitive habitats. Multiple consultants have characterized the site as unlikely to raise significant environmental concerns.

Site Characteristics

Engineering Consultant Assessment

"Looking at the aerial, this—you're not going to have many issues. This is actually a very attractive parcel for you and it is not going to raise red flags in any meaningful manner. Intuitively, the site is clean."

Environmental Consultant Assessment

"Since ENGIE is already building what you think they're building, this is probably cleared the pathway of any issues that would have been uncovered at that point in time."

Required Studies & Assessments

Phase I ESA Standard
Required for financing and equity backers. Identifies any historic contamination or environmental liabilities. For 45 acres, estimated at ~$5K boots-on-ground. Desktop version available at ~50% cost for initial screening.
Critical Issues Analysis Low Risk
Desktop review identifying any permitting concerns. Working document updated as studies complete. 2-3 month turnaround. Given adjacent solar development, expected to confirm clear pathway.
Wetlands Delineation Minimal Concern
Site is agricultural land in drought conditions. No apparent wetlands visible on aerial review. Desktop study likely sufficient initially; boots-on-ground if required by equity backer.
Threatened & Endangered Species Minimal Concern
Active agricultural use and adjacent solar development indicate no sensitive habitats. Desktop review expected to confirm. Can be bundled with wetlands study for efficiency.
Cultural Resources / Archaeology Low Risk
Desktop Arc Review (~2 weeks). No known burial sites or Native American reservations in area. ENGIE's adjacent development would have already flagged any cultural resources.
ALTA Survey Pending
Required for title insurance and financing. Documents property boundaries, easements, and encumbrances. Standard deliverable for project sale.
Local Permitting Favorable
Jones County has hosted renewable energy development for 7+ years. ENGIE's Anson Solar 1 (operational since 2020) and Anson Solar 2 (under construction) demonstrate established permitting pathways. County is familiar with utility-scale energy projects and has existing SOPs for approvals. Permitting expected to be limited to stormwater permitting (notice-level) once environmental due diligence confirms clean site.

Financing Considerations

ReDewable Inquiry

Regarding the studies being redone every six months—is that prior to submitting the application or all the way to NTP?

ESE Partners Response

"In the eyes of an equity backer, financier, or investment bank, they look at Phase I reports as good for only six months. If there's a lot of activity, they want reports updated—it's really just a check-the-box item. Sometimes we do the same Phase I four times over and nothing's changed, but that's their requirement. As soon as it hits that six-month threshold, they need it reactivated."

Key Takeaway

For a develop-and-sell strategy targeting NTP sale, timing of Phase I completion should align with anticipated buyer due diligence window. Desktop studies and CIA can be completed earlier; full Phase I ESA should be timed to remain current through sale process.

This assessment is based on preliminary consultations and desktop review. Final determinations will be made upon completion of formal studies. Environmental conditions are subject to change based on field verification.
