The Opportunity in Plain Terms

We're building what the Texas power grid desperately needs. Here's why it matters.

What We're Building

A massive battery facility that stores electricity when it's cheap and sells it when prices spike. Think of it like buying wholesale and selling retail—but for megawatts.

Why Texas Needs It

Texas has its own power grid (ERCOT) with no connections to other states. When demand surges, prices can spike 100x in minutes. Storage arbitrages this volatility.

Why This Location

Phantom Hill 345 kV is a high-voltage substation with excellent grid strength. Our independent studies confirm the connection can handle 300+ MW—a major technical milestone.

Why Partner With Us

We've done the hard early work: secured land, completed engineering studies, validated the grid connection. You're not buying an idea—you're buying a shovel-ready project.

Strategic Queue Position

ReDew Anson is currently preparing its ERCOT interconnection application. The Phantom Hill 345 kV substation already hosts ENGIE's Anson BAT project (a co-located battery supporting their solar generation), demonstrating the substation's proven capability for utility-scale storage. Our independent injection study was conducted with full awareness of existing committed capacity (~1.5 GW at the POI), confirming technical feasibility for our 300 MW addition. Battery storage capacity counts differently against grid limits than solar PV generation—a strategic advantage for our project positioning.

Common Questions

Notice to Proceed (NTP) is the milestone where a project is ready for construction to begin. It means: land is secured, permits are approved, engineering is complete, grid connection is confirmed, and an EPC contractor is selected. At NTP, the project has been de-risked—buyers pay a premium because they're acquiring a shovel-ready asset, not a speculative idea.
Because the alternative is worse. A large infrastructure fund trying to build this from scratch would need to: hire a development team, wait 3-4 years, navigate a saturated interconnection queue, risk project failure, and tie up management bandwidth. By acquiring at NTP, they skip the queue, deploy capital immediately, and let specialists (us) handle the messy early work. The $30-50M is a fraction of the $390-530M total project—and it's cheaper than the opportunity cost of waiting.
Typical acquirers include: energy infrastructure funds (Brookfield, BlackRock, Generate Capital), large IPPs (NextEra, Vistra, AES), utility-adjacent companies, and strategic buyers with grid positions. Notably, ENGIE already operates at the Phantom Hill substation with their co-located Anson BAT project—demonstrating active buyer interest in this specific location. These buyers have capital to deploy at scale and prefer buying de-risked projects over internal development.
Transmission upgrade costs: Final scope comes from ERCOT studies. Our estimate is $30-80M based on conductor-focused upgrades, but could vary. Queue dynamics: Other projects in the area affect upgrade allocation. Timeline: ERCOT study timing is partially outside our control. Market conditions: Battery storage revenues have declined significantly from 2023 peaks, affecting buyer valuations. We're mitigating through phased development (150 MW first) and conservative pricing expectations.
Three things: (1) 345 kV interconnection—high-voltage connections are more valuable and scarce; (2) Excellent grid strength—SCR supports 4,200 MVA capacity at Phantom Hill; (3) Proven substation—ENGIE's co-located Anson BAT project demonstrates the substation's capability for utility-scale storage. Phantom Hill is a quality connection point in a congested market.
ReDewable Energy Company, LLC is the development entity. Investment structure depends on timing and amount—could be direct equity in the project LLC, a note convertible at sale, or participation in a fund vehicle. All terms are negotiable and documented properly with counsel. We're seeking strategic capital partners who understand infrastructure development, not passive retail investors.
If market conditions prevent an NTP sale at acceptable terms, options include: (1) continue development through construction with a capital partner, (2) sell at an earlier stage (post-interconnection agreement), or (3) hold for market improvement. Land and permits retain value. The 345 kV interconnection position at Phantom Hill is inherently scarce—projects at quality substations eventually transact.
