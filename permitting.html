Environmental Due Diligence

Environmental complete. Favorable profile across the parcel.

ESE Partners delivered the Phase I ESA, Critical Issues Analysis, and Environmental Permit Matrix in February 2026. The 168-acre controlled property is directly adjacent to the operational ENGIE Anson Solar Farm. Studies confirm a favorable profile: no wetlands, no Waters of the US, no regulated floodplains, and no Superfund / RCRA / Brownfield sites within 1.5 miles. One historical REC (former reserve pit, ~1964) was identified on the far south edge of the southern parcel — outside the anticipated development footprint.

Findings

What the studies say.

Complete · Feb 2026
Phase I ESA
Standard due diligence. No HRECs, CRECs, de minimis, or significant data gaps. One REC identified (1964 reserve pit, far south edge) — outside development footprint; further investigation recommended. Valid through 8/22/2026; refresh aligned to financing window.
Complete · 2/26/2026
Critical Issues Analysis
No wetlands, no WOTUS, no regulated floodplains, no Superfund / RCRA / Brownfield sites within 1.5 miles. Monarch & Texas Horned Lizard habitat potential — BRE recommended. One dry hole on-site; one plugged hole just outside south boundary (20 ft setback recommended). Soils potentially corrosive — geotech recommended.
Complete · 2/25/2026
Environmental Permit Matrix
Stormwater Construction General Permit (notice-level) likely. Site is unincorporated — no municipal zoning. Road use agreement and tax abatement application through Jones County Judge's office recommended. DOD Siting Clearinghouse informal review required.
On-Site Gas Generation

Air permits in development.

On-site natural gas generation requires air permitting. Air permitting for the on-site generation is being scoped; responsibility is expected to sit with our power-generation partner under the final structure. Budget for scoping and coordination is contemplated within the seed-stage use of funds.
